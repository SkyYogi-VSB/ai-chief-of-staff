# 03 — Getting Things Done

Your CoS tracks four levels of work, from today's checklist to multi-month initiatives. Each level feeds into the morning brief so nothing falls through the cracks.

## The Four Levels

```
Workstreams (quarters)
    └── Projects (weeks to months)
        └── Actions (days to weeks)
            └── Todos (today)
```

### Todos — What You're Doing Today

A simple checkbox list, one file, organized by date.

```markdown
## 2024-01-15
- [x] Review contract draft from legal
- [x] Reply to partner on timeline question
- [ ] Prep slides for Thursday all-hands
- [ ] 1:1 with direct report — discuss Q2 goals

## 2024-01-16
- [ ] (rolled) Prep slides for Thursday all-hands
- [ ] Send updated forecast to finance
```

**Rules:**
- Max 5-7 items per day. If you have more, you're lying to yourself.
- At EOD, every unchecked item gets a decision: roll forward, discard, or convert to an Action.
- Never edit old dates. Roll forward or let it go.

### Actions — Work That Takes More Than a Day

Actions have an owner and a deadline. Two types:

- **Mine** — things I need to do
- **Chase** — things I'm waiting on from others

```markdown
## My Actions
- [ ] Draft Q2 OKRs (due: Jan 20) — next: outline in doc
- [ ] Finalize vendor contract (due: Jan 25) — next: legal review pending

## Chase Items
- [ ] Partner team: API spec review (asked: Jan 10, due: Jan 17)
- [ ] Finance: budget approval (asked: Jan 12, due: Jan 19)
```

**Rules:**
- Every action has a "next step" — the very next physical thing to do.
- Chase items track when you asked and when it's due. If overdue, your morning brief flags it.
- When complete, mark done. Don't delete — move to an archive section or let it scroll off.

### Projects — Multi-Week Efforts

Projects have a due date, a next step, and optionally a "blocked by."

```markdown
## Active Projects
| Project | Due | Next Step | Status |
|---------|-----|-----------|--------|
| Q2 planning deck | Jan 30 | Outline slides after OKRs finalize | On track |
| Vendor onboarding | Feb 15 | Waiting on legal signoff | Blocked |
| Team offsite planning | Mar 1 | Book venue — 3 options shortlisted | On track |
```

**Rules:**
- If a project has no next step, it's stalled. The morning brief will call it out.
- "Blocked" is a status, not an excuse. Your CoS should surface blocked projects daily until you unblock them or park them.
- Projects feed into the weekly review — that's where you update status and adjust timelines.

### Workstreams — The Big Picture

Workstreams are ongoing areas of responsibility. They don't have due dates — they have health.

```markdown
## Workstreams
| Workstream | Owner | Health | Key Milestone |
|------------|-------|--------|--------------|
| Product launch | You | Green | Beta: Feb 1 |
| Partner program | You + Partner | Yellow | Contract: Jan 25 (slipping) |
| Hiring | You | Red | 0/3 reqs filled, pipeline thin |
```

Health uses a simple traffic light:
- **Green** — on track, no intervention needed
- **Yellow** — at risk, needs attention this week
- **Red** — off track, needs intervention now

**Rules:**
- Update health weekly in the Friday review.
- Yellow is the action trigger. Red means you waited too long.
- Workstream health drives your priorities for the next week.

## How These Feed the Morning Brief

Your daily brief pulls from all four levels:

```
Good morning. Wednesday, January 15.

CALENDAR (5 meetings)
  09:00  1:1 with [Direct Report] (30 min)
  10:00  Product standup (30 min)
  ...

VIP EMAIL (2 messages)
  Your Manager — "Q2 planning — need your input by Thursday"
  Partner Lead — "Contract review — a few questions"

TODAY'S TODOS
  [ ] Review contract draft from legal
  [ ] Reply to partner on timeline question
  [ ] Prep slides for Thursday all-hands
  [ ] 1:1 with direct report

OVERDUE ACTIONS
  ⚠ Chase: Partner team API spec review — due yesterday, no response

ACTIVE PROJECTS
  Vendor onboarding — BLOCKED (legal signoff)

WORKSTREAM HEALTH
  Partner program — YELLOW (contract slipping)
```

Everything in one view. Two minutes to read. You know exactly what your day looks like.

## The Weekly Review Loop

Friday is when the system recalibrates:

1. **Walk each workstream** — what moved this week? Update health.
2. **Close completed actions** — mark done, celebrate wins.
3. **Add new actions** — from this week's emails, meetings, and conversations.
4. **Review projects** — update next steps, adjust timelines.
5. **Set next week's top 3** — what matters most? These become Monday's todo seeds.

The weekly review is the most important ritual. Skip it once and the system starts to drift. Skip it twice and you're back to chaos.

## Starting Small

You don't need all four levels on day one. Start with:

1. **Todos** — just today's checklist
2. **Morning brief** — calendar + email summary + todos
3. **EOD review** — close out todos, set tomorrow

After a week, add Actions. After two weeks, add Projects. Workstreams come when you have enough projects to need a higher-level view.

The system grows with your needs. Don't over-build upfront.
